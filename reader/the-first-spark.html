The first light of dawn had not yet touched the eastern peaks when Elara felt the pull. It was a sensation she had learned to recognize—a faint warmth in her chest, as if a single ember had been fanned to life.

She stood at the edge of the cliff, the wind pulling at her cloak, and watched the stars fade one by one. Below, the valley lay in shadow, but she could already see the outlines of the ancient trees, the silver thread of the river. This was the place the elders had spoken of. The place where the first spark had been kindled, centuries ago.

Elara closed her eyes and let the feeling grow. When she opened them again, the world had changed. Not in any way she could have explained to someone else—the rocks were still rocks, the sky still dark—but she knew, with a certainty that went beyond reason, that her life would not be the same after this day.

"So it begins," she whispered, and the wind carried her words away.
