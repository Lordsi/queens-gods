The archives were older than the kingdom. Elara ran her fingers along the spines of the books, feeling the faint hum of preserved magic. Some of these volumes had not been opened in generations.

She had come in search of answers. The spark had grown in the days since the cliff—no longer a single ember but a steady glow that pulsed in time with her heartbeat. The elders had given her only riddles. The books, she hoped, would give her truth.

She pulled a heavy tome from the shelf. The cover was plain leather, but the pages inside shimmered with script that shifted as she read. Whispers of the past, the librarian had called them. Echoes of those who had walked this path before.

Elara sat down at the reading desk and began to read. The first line made her breath catch. It was not in any language she had been taught. And yet she understood it perfectly.
